# Transitions, bumpers, stings & idents

Moving between shots and segments, and the branded interstitials that punctuate a programme.

## Scene transitions

- **Hard cut is the default.** In the large majority of moments, a plain cut is the right answer. Reserve an
  animated transition for moving between *major* chapters or sections, not every shot change.
- **Match cut** carries meaning across the join: match the **shape**, **colour**, or **action** between the two
  shots and the transition feels intentional and seamless.
  - *Graphic match* — same shape/size/placement bridges two shots.
  - *Colour match* — a shared colour links the scenes.
  - *Dynamic zoom* — push into or pull out of an element to move the viewer through.
- **Motion blur** on fast transitions simulates real movement — it smooths the shift and adds momentum/energy.
- **Easing + timing make it smooth.** Acceleration/deceleration (easing) plus the right duration and spacing are
  what read as "organic" rather than mechanical.

## Broadcast interstitials — the vocabulary

Branded pieces that structure a programme. Lengths are conventions, not laws:

| Piece | Length | What it is |
|---|---|---|
| **Bumper** | ~2–15 s | Short bridge between segments or into/out of a break; carries music + the show title/theme graphics. |
| **Sting** | ~5–10 s | Quick logo reveal at the end of a programme/video/ad — reminds the audience what they're watching. |
| **Ident** | ~10–30 s | Station/network/channel ID; a short brand ad. |
| **Sweep / stinger** | < 1 s | Dynamic animated wipe moving from one scene/segment to another, styled to the show's theme. |

A full video ident package typically chains: leader/intro → one or more bumpers → lower-third (name + title) →
transitions/wipes → endcard (CTA or credits).

## Rules for both

- **Carry the brand.** A transition or bumper is an identity moment — its colour, shape language, and sound are
  the brand. It should feel like the same system as the titles and plates, not a stock template.
- **Sound is half of it.** Sync the graphic hit to a beat or a sound design accent. A sweep or sting without an
  audio accent feels dead; matching cuts/reveals to the musical pulse fuses picture and sound.
- **Use sparingly.** Overusing transitions distracts and cheapens the edit. The more elaborate the move, the
  rarer it should be — big moves mark big boundaries.
- **Tempo = emotion.** Quick cuts heighten tension; longer holds feel calm and considered. Rhythm is "visual
  music" — shot durations form a pattern that sets mood as much as content does. Speed ramps (gradual or abrupt
  speed changes) let the motion match the dynamics of the track.
